Understanding Customer Preferences & Buying Patterns in Online Grocery Platforms

Tools: Excel, SPSS, Python | Techniques: MLR, Conjoint Analysis, Market Basket Analysis

Led a comprehensive data-driven study to understand key drivers of customer satisfaction and buying behaviour across online grocery platforms such as Blinkit, Zepto, Instamart and BigBasket. The project combined Multiple Linear Regression, Conjoint Analysis, and Market Basket Analysis to generate actionable insights for quick-commerce strategy.

🔍 Key Contributions & Outcomes:

Built an MLR model (R² = 0.824) to identify what impacts customer satisfaction the most—highlighting delivery speed, price fairness, product availability, and delivery fee policies as top predictors.

Conducted Conjoint Analysis on 40 profiles to understand customer trade-offs, finding 10-minute delivery, free delivery above ₹399, and high product availability as highest-utility combinations.

Performed Market Basket Analysis on 55 transactions, discovering strong item associations such as Shampoo → Conditioner (Lift 3.36) and Paneer → Coffee (Lift 4.58), enabling data-backed bundling strategies.

Identified three behavioural customer segments using item association patterns—Family Restockers, Modern Breakfast Consumers, and Impulse Buyers.

Delivered managerial recommendations on delivery optimization, pricing strategy, inventory management, and personalised recommendations for quick-commerce platforms.


📌 Impact:
Provided a 360° view of customer expectations and buying behaviours, helping streamline operations, enhance app UX, and design profitable product bundles for online grocery businesses.
